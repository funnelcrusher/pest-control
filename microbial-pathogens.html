    What are microbial pathogens and how do they help in natural pest control?

    Microbial pathogens are disease-causing agents, such as bacteria, viruses, and fungi which can be used to naturally control pests in agriculture. They work by introducing the pathogen into a population of pests, either by spraying them or releasing them into the environment, which then causes an infection that kills the pests.

    Are there any risks associated with using microbial pathogens for natural pest control?

    Yes, there are some risks associated with using microbial pathogens for natural pest control. The introduction of a pathogen may cause it to spread beyond its intended target or become resistant to treatment over time due to changes in its genetic makeup. Additionally, some microbial pathogens may harm beneficial organisms or even humans if not carefully monitored and managed.

    How effective is natural pest control compared to chemical pesticides?

    Natural pest control methods can be effective at controlling certain types of pests but may not be as effective as chemical pesticides when it comes to large-scale infestations or rapid elimination of a particular species of pest. Additionally, chemical pesticides often have negative environmental consequences while natural methods tend to be more eco-friendly and sustainable in the long term.
